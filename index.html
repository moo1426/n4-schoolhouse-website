NO. 4 SCHOOLHOUSE, INC.

 

HISTORY

 In 1790 the Town of Barre formed districts and the building of schoolhouses began.  The first mention of District No. 4 was in 1788, when it was voted to choose a committee to regulate the schools in the several school quadrants.  In 1790, the location was chosen for Ward 4.

 

 The first No. 4 schoolhouse seems to have been completed in 1792 with materials donated by the District’s members.  The initial structure proved inadequate, and in or about 1796 a brick structure was built near the site of the first schoolhouse.  Attendance at this time rose to between 30 and 60 pupils.  The brick schoolhouse lasted until 1881-1882.

 

 It is noted in the records regarding the second schoolhouse that the Bible was required to be read, a common water pail was used by all students, and their play area was a cow pasture.

 

 The School Committee of 1883-1884 decided to build a new No. 4 schoolhouse, which is the building now standing.  It was voted to raise $1,200.00 to build it, and the building committee consisting of James F. Davis, H. H. Brigham, and P. Mirick Harwood was formed on May 14, 1883.  James H. Potter was chosen as the builder and was paid $825.00 to construct the schoolhouse.

 

 In 1896 the No. 9 District schoolhouse was closed and those children then attended the No. 4 School.

 

 The years 1879-1901 show an average class size of 30-35 students. There was one teacher for the entire class - with no help.

 

 There was a family atmosphere about the School.  The students and families of the District reflected the impact of immigration with English, Irish, and some Italian families.

 

 In 1918 the pupils of No. 4 School aided in the World War I effort by gathering peach stones, nuts, and shells for use in making gas masks.

 

 There was a high turnover of teachers with the exception of a few steadfast ones due to the large size of the class and low pay.  In 1887-1889 Gertrude Ball received $8.00 a week.  A female teacher had to be single in order to teach at the School.  Silas Partridge is the first known teacher and in 1929-1930 Katherine Hanley was the last.

 

 The report of the School Committee in 1930 reported the closing of the school, in order to reduce operating expenses.  The children were then transported to the Center School, with the No. 4 School being kept for possible overflow.  The No. 4 School was the last of the district schools to be closed.

 

 The overflow of the Center School never occurred.

 

 On May 7, 1937, a meeting was held at the home of Mrs. Alfred Cummings for the purpose of determining whether there was any interest in purchasing the No. 4 School property.  On June 7, 1937, at a meeting of the neighborhood, the decision was made to purchase the property.  A constitution and by-laws were established and the No. 4 Community Club was formed.  On December 28, 1937, the No. 4 School property was sold to the No. 4 Community Club.

 

 For many years, the No. 4 Community Club held reunions and community gatherings and preserved the building.  The Club’s activities and preservation efforts were funded by the members’ dues and contributions, as well as by fairs and ham and bean suppers.  There have been many social gatherings, including Halloween and Christmas parties, which are still on-going.

 

 The No. 4 Community Club was incorporated as a non-profit corporation in 1978.  In 1989 the name of the organization was changed from No. 4 Community Club, Inc. to No. 4 Schoolhouse, Inc. and 501(c)(3) non-profit status was granted by the Internal Revenue Service.

 

 On June 22, 1988, the building was accepted for inclusion on the National Register of Historic Places.

  

 Over the years, the building fell into disrepair and there were not enough members or funds to repair it.  However, between 2013 and 2016, through the hard work of many members, the receipt of two grants, and various fundraisers, the No. 4 Schoolhouse was restored.

 

 A grand re-opening ceremony was held on September 19, 2015 with many in attendance, including a former student.

No. 4 Schoolhouse is a historic one room schoolhouse located on Farrington Road in Barre Massachusetts. In 2013-2015 it underwent a massive restoration, repairing the interior and exterior to its original condition and layout. Number 4 Schoolhouse is the home to the Number 4 Community Club, which host events throughout the year, such as a children Christmas and Halloween Party, For upcoming events please look at the calendar below. More information about the events can be found in the News section. Feel free to browse around the site to learn more about the No. 4 Schoolhouse.
